# Surprising Findings from the Emergent Ontology Corpus Analysis

## Introduction

Two independent corpora were classified into the twenty-seven positions of Emergent Ontology, using both Claude and GPT-4 as independent classifiers.

Corpus A drew from Universal Dependencies treebanks: nearly twenty thousand clauses across forty-one languages, covering news, Wikipedia, legal text, literature, and web content.

Corpus B was specialist English: one thousand clauses from quantum physics papers on arXiv, two thousand from philosophy — Greek, Taoist, and Stoic traditions — and five hundred ninety-five from biblical wisdom literature.

Both corpora were classified by two models independently, and only consensus clauses — where both models agreed — were retained. That's about a forty percent agreement rate.

What emerged was a set of surprising structural findings that nobody predicted in advance.

---

## 1. The Third Triad Dominates Philosophical Text

Here is the first surprise.

In everyday language, the Structure triad dominates. Forty-five percent of clauses in Corpus A fall into Segmenting, Connecting, or Synthesizing. The most common single cell is CON(Binding, Link) — basic connection-making — at twenty-seven percent.

But in the specialist corpus — philosophy, physics, wisdom literature — the entire distribution inverts.

The Significance triad takes over at fifty-three percent. The single most common cell becomes SUP(Tracing, Paradigm), at 15.4%. That's the superposition operator targeting crystallizing patterns. Second is SUP(Binding, Lens) at 12.6%. Third is ALT(Unraveling, Paradigm) at 12.0%.

This means that philosophical and scientific text lives overwhelmingly in the meaning-making, value-shifting, interpretation-reframing zone of the ontology. It doesn't just describe things or connect them. It holds contradictions, reframes paradigms, and operates on patterns rather than discrete entities.

---

## 2. Object Grain is the Strongest Signal — Not Domain

This one is genuinely counterintuitive.

Of the three axes — Mode, Domain, and Object — you might expect Domain to be the strongest discriminator. After all, it captures the level of reality: existence versus structure versus significance.

But no. The Object axis — which captures what grain of target a transformation addresses: conditions, entities, or patterns — produces the strongest geometric signal in embedding space.

In the specialist corpus, Object achieves a z-score of +7.87 standard deviations. Domain reaches +5.68. Mode reaches +3.63.

Object is not just slightly stronger — it's dramatically stronger. And this signal amplifies by a factor of 2.9x when you move from everyday text to philosophical text.

What this means is that the most robustly distinguishable semantic dimension in natural language is not *how* you transform, or *at what level*, but *whether you are targeting background conditions, concrete entities, or emerging patterns.*

---

## 3. Domain and Object Couple Tightly in Abstract Reasoning

In the Universal Dependencies corpus — everyday language — the Domain axis and the Object axis are nearly orthogonal, separated by 66 degrees. They operate independently.

But in the specialist corpus, something dramatic happens. That angle collapses to 32 degrees. Domain and Object become tightly coupled. At the same time, Mode and Object achieve near-perfect independence at 78 degrees.

In abstract reasoning — philosophy, physics, wisdom — the level of reality you operate on fundamentally constrains what kind of target you can engage. You cannot freely choose to target conditions or patterns independently of whether you're working at the existence level, the structure level, or the significance level. They become locked together.

This coupling does not exist in everyday language. It emerges specifically when text becomes conceptually dense.

---

## 4. The Helix Direction Reverses

The Emergent Ontology predicts a directional dependency called the helix — a presupposition ordering from Existence to Structure to Significance.

In the Universal Dependencies corpus, the entropy asymmetry confirms the predicted direction: Mode is upstream of Domain. The asymmetry is +0.134 bits.

But in the specialist corpus, the direction reverses. The asymmetry flips to -0.067 bits. Domain becomes upstream of Mode.

In everyday language, *how* you transform determines *what level* you work at. In philosophical language, the level determines the mode.

This is a fundamental finding: the helix's information flow is register-dependent. The causal direction of ontological presupposition depends on what kind of thinking you're doing.

---

## 5. Axes Decouple Only in Dense Text

In the Universal Dependencies corpus, Mode and Domain show a borderline coupling — an Adjusted Rand Index of 0.185. In the specialist corpus, this drops to 0.096. The coupling is halved. All three axis pairs pass the independence criterion only in the specialist corpus.

This suggests something remarkable: the three axes of Emergent Ontology achieve their cleanest independence specifically in text that does sustained conceptual work.

The Significance-dominant register — where people are actively reframing and superposing meanings — is where the dimensional structure is most clearly realized. Everyday language blurs the axes. Philosophical language separates them.

---

## 6. Models Disagree Most About Significance

When Claude and GPT-4 classify the same clauses, their largest disagreements cluster in the third triad.

The single most contested cell is SUP(Tracing, Paradigm) — the very cell that dominates the specialist corpus. Claude sees it at 16.3%; GPT-4 sees it at 11.1%. A 5.2% gap — the largest in the entire analysis.

Six of the top eight divergences across both corpora involve Significance operators.

This means the third triad is where semantic classification is most ambiguous, most dependent on unstated assumptions about what counts as *meaning* versus *structure*. It's the frontier of interpretive uncertainty for language models themselves.

---

## 7. Predicted Geometry Fails for Significance

The Emergent Ontology predicts that the Domain axis should follow geometric (asymmetric) spacing. The distance from Existence to Structure should be about 4.8x greater than from Structure to Significance.

The actual data shows the opposite.

In the specialist corpus, Existence and Significance are equidistant from Structure, both at about 0.16 in embedding space. The predicted ratio of 4.8 comes out at 1.6.

In the Universal Dependencies corpus, it's even more inverted: the ratio is 0.64. Significance is actually *closer* to Existence than to Structure.

This challenges the hierarchical model. Significance does not scale as "higher complexity." It appears to occupy a symmetrical, orthogonal alternative to Existence — not a further step up from Structure.

---

## 8. Pattern Targets Dominate the Significance Triad

In specialist philosophical and physics text, the Protogon entity type — pattern-dominant targets — accounts for nearly half of all Significance triad clauses. Roughly 47%.

Emanon (condition-level targets) are almost absent at 2%. Holon (entity-level targets) sit at about 22%.

Compare this with the Universal Dependencies corpus, where Entity-level targets dominate.

Philosophy and physics don't operate on things. They don't even primarily operate on conditions or contexts. They operate on crystallizing patterns and paradigms.

The Significance triad, in conceptually dense text, is a zone of pattern-on-pattern transformation. Superposing paradigms, reframing crystallizing structures, recursively applying meaning to meaning.

---

## Conclusion

The corpus analysis reveals that the third triad — Significance — is where the most unexpected and revealing structure lives.

The everyday gravity well of language sits in Structure: connecting things, segmenting space. But when text becomes philosophically or scientifically engaged, gravity shifts to Significance, axes decouple, coupling patterns change, and the helix reverses direction.

The third triad is not merely an extension of the first two. It represents a qualitatively different regime of semantic operation where the ontology's geometry both sharpens and transforms.

This is perhaps the strongest evidence that the twenty-seven positions of Emergent Ontology capture something real about the deep structure of meaning-making — something that only fully reveals itself when language is pushed into its most demanding registers.
