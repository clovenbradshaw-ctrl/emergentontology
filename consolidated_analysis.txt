==========================================================================
  CONSOLIDATED FINDINGS
==========================================================================

  1. THE STRUCTURE IS REAL AND REPLICABLE
     Both corpora independently produce significant z-scores on all
     three axes, perfect monotonicity, moderate inter-model agreement,
     and nearly identical full 27-cell z-scores (~16 SDs).

  2. SPECIALIST TEXT AMPLIFIES THE SIGNAL
     Every axis z-score is higher in Corpus B. The specialist corpus
     achieves the Mode equal-steps prediction and full axis independence
     that the UD corpus misses. Register-controlled, conceptually rich
     text produces cleaner ontological geometry.

  3. THE DISTRIBUTION SHIFTS BUT THE STRUCTURE HOLDS
     Corpus A lives in the CON/INS/SUP gravity well (Structure-dominant,
     Entity-prone). Corpus B shifts to SUP/ALT/CON (Significance-
     dominant, Pattern-prone). The 27-cell structure accommodates this
     shift — different cells populate, but the coordinate system remains
     intact.

  4. THE HELIX DIRECTION IS REGISTER-SENSITIVE
     Mode→Domain in everyday text; Domain→Mode in meaning-dense text.
     The helix dependency describes typical information flow, not a
     universal constraint. The coupling strength (Q1/Q2 ARI) halves in
     register-controlled text.

  5. DOMAIN × OBJECT COUPLING EMERGES IN SPECIALIST TEXT
     The UD corpus shows Domain and Object as nearly orthogonal (66°).
     The specialist corpus couples them tightly (32°). In philosophical
     and scientific writing, what level of reality you're operating on
     strongly constrains what kind of target you're engaging. This is
     plausible: quantum physics doesn't produce many "Entity × Existence"
     clauses — it operates on patterns in significance.
